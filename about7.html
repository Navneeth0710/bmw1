About Sedan Series

BMW 7

Engine (some variants): 2993 cc 6-cylinder (diesel in some markets), with high performance: up to ~ 375.48 bhp & 520 Nm torque (as per one spec sheet).
Seating / Capacity: 5-seater luxury sedan; boot ≈ 540 litres.
Price / Segment: Flagship — “full-size luxury sedan” — highest in sedan line within BMW.
Ideal Use: Chauffeured rides, executive transport, long-distance comfort, status/brand prestige, maximum comfort + luxury.
Premium Features:
As flagship, 7 Series often includes top-end comfort, advanced safety and convenience features, premium interiors, high noise isolation, large cabin space, and smooth ride — befitting a luxury sedan.

BMW 2 Series Gran Coupé

Starting price in India (ex-showroom): ~ ₹ 45.30 lakh.
Engines / Fuel type: Petrol (for recent models).
Segment: Compact / subcompact-executive sedan (a more affordable entry into BMW sedans).
Ideal for: Urban commuting, first-time luxury sedan buyers, those seeking a sporty-compact sedan feel without premium-sedan size.
Note: Detailed engine-specs (power, torque, etc.) vary by variant and are often modest compared to larger models — emphasis is on affordability and everyday usability.

BMW 3 Series

Engine options: Petrol and Diesel — commonly 1998 cc (and others depending on variant) in India.
Price range (India): ~ ₹ 60.45 lakh (base LWB) and upwards depending on trim.
Known for: Driving dynamics + practicality + comfort; Carries BMW’s “sport-sedan + premium” balance.
Typical use-cases: Individual executives, small families, city + highway driving, “first proper BMW sedan” for many buyers.
Important specs (varies a lot with trim/engine, but as per some listings):
Top-end (global/manual listing) shows engines like 2998 cc, with high performance metrics.
Offers a balance of performance, comfort, and practicality — making it one of BMW’s most popular sedans globally.
