About M series

BMW M3

The M3 is the performance-tuned version of BMW’s 3-series sedan, developed by BMW’s motorsport division.
In many markets: M3 comes with a powerful petrol engine and sporty tuning, delivering strong acceleration and “M-car” driving dynamics.
As with other M cars, it emphasizes a balance of performance, handling, and everyday usability — but with clear “sports-sedan” bias

BMW M5

M5 uses a ~4395 cc petrol engine (often paired with hybrid/plug-in hybrid in latest versions).
Power output: around 717 bhp (per one spec listing) with very high torque figures.
Performance: 0-100 km/h acceleration in roughly 3.5 seconds; top speed (in performance configuration) — very high (on par with many super-sedans).
Despite being a “super-salon,” M5 retains luxurious comfort and advanced tech — making it a “dual personality” car: daily driver + performance machine.

BMW M8

M8 is the flagship performance-coupé / gran-coupé of BMW’s M lineup — combining supercar-level performance with luxury grand touring capability.
Engine: 4.4-litre twin-turbo V8 (in competition-spec) delivering about 617 hp.
Design & Character: As a grand tourer + performance car hybrid — it’s as much about refined comfort and style as it is about speed.

BMW M2

M2 is the “entry-level” performance coupe in BMW’s M lineup — smaller and more compact than M3/M5/M8 but offering strong performance and the M-car feel.
In terms of positioning, it appeals to buyers who want a sporty coupe with BMW M heritage — but in a more accessible/smaller package compared to flagship M-models
