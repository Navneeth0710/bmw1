About i Series

BMW i4

Overview & Purpose
The i4 is a fully-electric compact executive “Gran Coupe” / 5-door liftback from BMW.
It’s aimed at buyers who want a balance of everyday usability, sporty character, and electric mobility.

Specs & Performance

Power ranges from ~335 hp in base rear-wheel-drive eDrive models to ~536 hp in top AWD “M” versions.
Battery capacity: ~70.2 kWh – 83.9 kWh (depending on variant).
Driving range: ~483 km – 590 km per full charge (as claimed in some markets).
Top speed around 190 km/h (depending on trim).

Why Consider i4

It delivers the electric-car advantages (quiet, zero-tailpipe emissions) while still feeling like a “real” BMW.
Its Gran Coupe / liftback body gives more practicality than a typical EV “sedan”.
Good balance of performance, comfort and everyday usability among BMW’s EV lineup.

BMW i5

Overview & Purpose

The i5 is the all-electric version of BMW’s 5 Series — a larger, more luxurious electric sedan meant to replace/augment the traditional 5-Series with EV powertrain.
It aims at drivers who want electric mobility without sacrificing comfort, space, or luxury.

Specs & Performance

The lineup includes:


eDrive40 (single-motor, RWD)
xDrive40 (dual-motor, AWD)
M60 xDrive (high-performance AWD)
The M60 xDrive offers ~593 hp and strong torque, delivering very strong performance.
The claimed driving range is around ~516 km (in some market data) depending on variant & usage.
Battery pack is large (approx ~84 kWh usable depending on spec) for a luxury sedan.

Why Consider i5

Great balance: EV benefits + spacious cabin + premium luxury — a strong alternative to traditional 5-Series sedans.
Good for those needing more rear-seat space / comfort (longer wheelbase, sedan body) than smaller EV sedans.
If performance matters — M60 AWD variant delivers serious power along with EV efficiency.

BMW i8

(Hybrid Sports Car — legacy model)

Overview & Purpose

The i8 was a plug-in hybrid sports-car from BMW’s “i-series” (though it’s hybrid, not purely electric).
It was meant to show what a futuristic, high-performance, sustainable sports car can be — combining combustion and electric power for performance + efficiency.
Production began 2014; i8 was one of BMW’s most futuristic and iconic designs (scissor doors, aerodynamic styling, hybrid-powertrain).

Performance & Specs

Combined system (gas + electric) output ~362 PS (or higher depending on model version) + a hybrid electric motor.
Top speed electronically limited to 250 km/h.
In all-electric mode: modest performance (EV mode top ~120 km/h), main benefit was efficiency & reduced emissions for short trips.
Lightweight & aerodynamic design (low drag coefficient ~0.26) helped with efficiency and sporty handling.

Why i8 Still Matters

It was a “halo” car for BMW — blending hybrid-efficiency with sports-car aesthetics & performance.
Showed BMW’s vision of combining sustainability with performance — influencing future “i-series” EV development.
For many enthusiasts, it’s still admired for its design, innovation, and pioneering hybrid-sports-EV concept.
