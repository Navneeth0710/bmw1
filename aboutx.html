About X series

BMW X3

Engine / Powertrains (for recent generation)
Petrol / hybrid 2.0 L (B48) – ~135 kW / 290 Nm, 0-100 km/h ~8.4 s.
Petrol 2.0 L (B48) stronger tune – ~185 kW / 350 Nm, 0-100 km/h ~6.4–6.6 s.
Petrol 3.0 L inline-6 (M40i) – ~265 kW (or 285 kW in recent) / 495–500 Nm, 0–100 km/h ~4.9 s.
Diesel 2.0 L (B47) or 3.0 L (B57) variants also available (e.g. 3.0 L diesel: ~195 kW / 620 Nm, 0–100 km/h ~5.7 s)
Performance variant BMW X3 M / M-Competition: up to 353 kW (600 Nm), 0–100 km/h ~4.0 s (or 3.7 s for Comp)

BMW X5

Engine / Powertrains & Performance
Standard 3.0 L turbo-6 petrol (xDrive40i) — power ~375 bhp.
More powerful versions are available (depending on variant).
Typical mileage ~12 kmpl (petrol) per CarWale data.

BMW X6

Engine / Power / Performance
Base (or standard) 3.0 L engine — 2998 cc petrol (or diesel depending on variant).
Example variant: 3.0 L turbo inline-6 with 48V mild-hybrid — ~375 hp.
Higher-end variant (e.g. 4.4 L twin-turbo V8 in some trims) — ~523 hp, 553 lb-ft torque.
Even more performance-oriented “M” trims — up to ~617 hp (for X6 M)

BMW iX

Powertrain / Electric / Performance
Fully electric SUV — uses dual-motor xDrive (all-wheel drive) across variants.
Battery & Range: one version uses ~105.2 kWh battery pack offering up to ~324 miles per charge (EPA estimate) or more under ideal conditions.
Charging: DC fast-charging supported — can recharge 10–80% in about 35 min with 200 kW charger.
Performance: certain trims offer up to ~610 hp (in high-end version), making it competitive among luxury electric SUVs.
